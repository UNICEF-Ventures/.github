1. Our Pledge
The UNICEF Venture Fund builds and invests in open source technology as Digital Public Goods — software, data, and AI models that are openly licensed, free of harmful components, and built to serve the public interest. The contributors, maintainers, and community members who gather in this GitHub organization share a common purpose: to create technology that works for every child.
We pledge to make participation in this community a welcoming, safe, and equitable experience for everyone — regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.
We are especially committed to lowering barriers to participation for contributors from the Global South, from low-resource environments, and from communities whose needs our technology is designed to serve. Equity of access and voice is central to how we build.

2. Our Values
Our work is shaped by the following commitments, grounded in both open source principles and UNICEF's mission:

Open by default. We share knowledge, processes, and decisions openly. We do not hoard information or create unnecessary barriers to contribution.
Public interest first. Our work exists to benefit children and communities, not to extract value from them. Decisions are guided by impact, not prestige.
Equity over efficiency. We actively reduce barriers for contributors from underrepresented regions and backgrounds. Diversity of perspective makes our work stronger.
Do no harm. We are mindful that the technology we build can affect vulnerable populations, including children. We take this responsibility seriously in all contributions.
Accountability and repair. We take responsibility for our actions. When harm occurs, we prioritize repair over punishment wherever it is safe and possible to do so.


3. Encouraged Behaviors
We strive to:

Demonstrate empathy and kindness toward other community members
Respect differing opinions, viewpoints, and lived experiences
Give and receive constructive feedback gracefully
Accept responsibility when our contributions cause harm, apologize to those affected, and learn from the experience
Focus on what is best not just for ourselves, but for the community and the people our work is designed to serve
Credit others' work appropriately — including open source dependencies, upstream projects, and contributors
Communicate asynchronously with care, recognizing that contributors may work across time zones and in their non-primary language
Be patient and welcoming with newcomers and contributors from different technical backgrounds


4. Unacceptable Behaviors
The following are prohibited in all spaces governed by this Code of Conduct:

Harassment. Sustained, unwelcome attention; violating expressed boundaries; continuing contact after a clear request to stop.
Character attacks. Insulting or demeaning comments directed at a person or group, including dismissing contributions based on background or identity.
Discrimination or stereotyping. Characterizing anyone's personality or behavior based on immutable identities or traits.
Sexualization. Use of sexualized language or imagery, or any unwelcome sexual attention in community spaces.
Threats or endangerment. Causing, encouraging, or threatening violence or harm toward any person or group.
Doxxing. Sharing or acting on someone's personal or private information without their explicit permission.
Misleading identity. Impersonating another community member or using a false identity to evade enforcement.
Bad faith contributions. Deliberately introducing malicious, harmful, or deceptive content into repositories in this organization.
Exploitation of the mission. Using the UNICEF name, brand, or community resources for personal commercial gain not sanctioned by UNICEF.


5. Scope
This Code of Conduct applies within all spaces managed under the UNICEF Venture Fund GitHub organization (github.com/unicef), including:

Code repositories — commits, pull requests, issues, and code review comments
GitHub Discussions, wikis, and project boards
README files, documentation, and written content within repositories
Automated systems such as GitHub Actions and bots operating within the organization

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the UNICEF Venture Fund in any public space — for example, using an official email address, posting via an official account, or acting as a named representative for a repository or project.
This Code of Conduct applies to all contributors regardless of affiliation — including UNICEF staff, Venture Fund portfolio companies, consultants, volunteers, and external contributors.

6. Reporting an Incident
Not every conflict is a Code of Conduct violation. Many tensions can be resolved through respectful conversation. When you are unsure, you are always welcome to report and let the maintainers assess.
If you experience or witness a violation, please report it by emailing:

Please include:

Your contact information (or indicate if you wish to report anonymously)
The names or usernames of those involved
A description of the incident, including direct links or screenshots where available
Any relevant context or background

All reports will be handled with discretion. Reporters' identities will be kept confidential to the extent possible while enabling a fair investigation.

7. Enforcement
Reports are reviewed and acted upon by the UNICEF Venture Fund Maintainers. Maintainers will:

Acknowledge receipt of a report promptly
Investigate all reports seriously, reviewing available evidence and speaking with relevant parties where appropriate
Keep investigations and enforcement actions confidential
Communicate outcomes to the reporting party in a timely manner

Enforcement Ladder
| Level | Trigger | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Correction | Single or minor violation | Private written notice; acknowledgement or apology may be requested |
| 2. Warning | Repeated minor violation or a more serious first incident | Formal written warning; notice that further violations escalate |
| 3. Temporary suspension | Pattern of violations or a serious single incident | Temporary removal of access to some or all community spaces; conditions for return specified in writing |
| 4. Permanent ban | Sustained violations after earlier steps, or a violation so serious that community safety requires immediate removal | Permanent removal from all UNICEF Venture Fund GitHub organization spaces |
Maintainers retain discretion to skip steps when severity warrants it. Enforcement decisions may be appealed by emailing *(reporting email — coming soon)* with the subject line "Appeal".

8. Conflict of Interest
Any maintainer who is personally involved in a reported incident, or who has a close relationship with any party, must recuse themselves from the investigation and enforcement process for that incident.
